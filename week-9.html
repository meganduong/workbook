Hunt N Gathers

The Penguins Mirror

Penguins Mirror (2015) by Daniel Rozin is an interactive kinetic installation composed of 450 black-and-white stuffed penguins, each mounted on a motor. Using a video camera to track the viewer’s movement, the penguins rotate in unison to form a dynamic, low-resolution mirror that reflects the viewer’s silhouette in real time.

I think it is always interesting to see animatronic/ robotic kids toys used in a different content. While the penguins bring a hint of whimsy and playfulness to the piece, its mundane roll of rotating and reflecting the users’ silhouette reduces the toy down to its basic inner mechanical workings and purpose.

Study for Fifteen Points

Composed of fifteen mechanised LED points suspended in space, Study for Fifteen Points(2015) by Random International is a light sculpture that mimics the motion of a walking human figure.

Interestingly, despite the absence of a visible body, viewers are able to instinctively recognise the illuminated form as human, highlighting the brain’s ability to synthesise sparse visual cues into coherent representations.

I find this interesting as I find this similar to how biometric technology can recognise the identity of someone from just a number of points on the face.
